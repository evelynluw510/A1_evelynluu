Weekly Screen Time Breakdown: Where My Hours Go

Weekly screen time

My screen time breakdown by app


My weekly screen time record shows how I share up my time across various apps. TikTok is at the top of the list, taking up over 18 hours of my week, followed by Messenger at 10. Instagram takes up three hours of my time, whereas Safari takes up eight hours, probably from surfing and research. Finally, at one hour, WhatsApp experiences the least amount of usage. It's interesting to see where my screen time is going, as I've spent 40 hours on just five apps alone.




Average Pickups

Number of total pickups


TikTok is by far the most used app, since I open it 130 times a week, according to my Average Pickups chart. This implies that anytime I unlock my phone, I have a strong habit of automatically checking TikTok.Safari comes in second with 96 pickups, suggesting that it is frequently used for web searches or browsing. Weekly average screen time

Two-week comparision



Tracking my screen time over several weeks gives me important information about how my app usage changes. My usage decreased from 23 hours in the first week to 18 hours in the second, while TikTok is still the most popular app. This decline suggests that I might have consciously tried to spend less time scrolling. However, Messenger increased from 7 to 10 hours, suggesting that I might have been more active in messaging or chats during the second week. Instagram and WhatsApp usage did not vary, remaining at 3 and 1 hours, respectively, while Safari usage stayed steady at 8 hours.



Overall, the pattern shows that I've been using TikTok less, but I've been using communication applications more or less. I may have a better understanding of my digital habits and figure out whether I want to keep adjusting my screen time in order to achieve a better balance by going over these weekly revisions.
